Power for you! — The Treaties of the EU

A workshop on the treaties that form the foundation of the European Union — discovered in a playful way, through a quiz and a board game.

Authors: Barbara Petersen, Renate Kronemann, Sabine Perisic, Achim Kolbe-Dierks

AudienceAges 50+ TypeWorkshop + board game

About the Workshop

What are the treaties on which the EU is built? Participants discover them in a light, playful way: first a quiz, then the board game «Power for you!», and finally a guided discussion on how EU law touches everyday life.

How the Workshop Unfolds

  1. 1

    Quiz: "Treaty or Fiction?"

    Read a sentence and guess whether it comes from a real EU treaty or from a work of fiction.

  2. 2

    Input 1 — The treaties (introduction)

    An overview of the EU treaties: the timeline, and the difference between primary and secondary law.

  3. 3

    Board game "Power for you!"

    Players move across fields of knowledge, action and gifts, discovering the treaties as they go.

  4. 4

    Input 2 — The treaties (advanced)

    The key principles and how EU laws are decided (the different majorities).

  5. 5

    Discussion

    An example from your own life where EU law has enriched you.

The Treaties — a Timeline

From the Treaty of Paris to the Treaty of Lisbon: the steps that built the Union.

  1. 1951

    Treaty of Paris

    Establishes the European Coal and Steel Community (ECSC).

  2. 1957

    Treaties of Rome

    Establish the European Economic Community (EEC).

  3. 1985

    Schengen Agreement

    Regulates the free movement of persons within the EU.

  4. 1992

    Maastricht Treaty

    The founding treaty of the European Union (Treaty on European Union).

  5. 1997

    Treaty of Amsterdam

    More rights for citizens and a stronger internal policy.

  6. 2001

    Treaty of Nice

    Prepares the enlargement to the countries of Eastern Europe.

  7. 2009

    Treaty of Lisbon

    Makes the EU more democratic, transparent and effective. The EU's current treaty basis.

Primary and Secondary Law

The treaties are the EU's «primary law»; everything else must respect them.

Primary law

The EU's supreme law: the founding treaties on which the Union is based.

Secondary law

All other EU law (laws, directives, regulations). It must comply with primary law and may not contradict it.

TEU — Treaty on European Union

In force since 1993. The EU's fundamental values, competences and institutions — its «constitutional framework».

TFEU — Treaty on the Functioning of the EU

In force since 1957. The internal market, agriculture, the environment, competition… a «day-to-day treaty».

Charter of Fundamental Rights

Legally binding since the Treaty of Lisbon. Sets out the fundamental rights of individuals within the EU.

Key Principles & How Laws Are Decided

Principle of conferral

The EU may act only in the areas for which the Member States have conferred competence on it (Art. 5(1) TEU).

Principle of subsidiarity

The EU acts only if the objectives can be better achieved at EU level than by the individual States (Art. 5(3) TEU).

Qualified majority

Most decisions: 55% of the States (at least 15) representing at least 65% of the EU population.

Unanimity

For sensitive areas: taxation, new members, foreign & security policy, the EU budget, treaty changes.

Simple majority

Mainly for procedural decisions (Art. 238 TFEU).

The Board Game «Power for you!»

Playing in groups, participants move across three kinds of fields and discover the treaties step by step.

Fields of knowledge

Knowledge cards with questions about the treaties and EU law.

Fields of action

Action cards: a small task or decision to carry out together.

Gift fields

Gift cards: small bonuses that help you along the way.

It all starts with the quiz «Treaty or Fiction?»: read a quote and guess whether it comes from a real EU treaty or from a story (Star Wars, The Little Prince…). A fun way to enter the world of the treaties.
