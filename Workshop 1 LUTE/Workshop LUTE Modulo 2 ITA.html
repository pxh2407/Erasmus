Workshop 1

Organs of the European Union

A participatory journey to discover European Institutions through role-play, group work and civic reflection.

Coordinatrici: Lilla Trischitta e Pina Mastroieni

Tutor: Antonio Catalfamo

Duration 2 – 2.5 hours Participants 20 – 25 Audience Adults / Seniors
Simulazione Esercitazioni

General Objectives

  1. 1Develop civic awareness of the role of EU Institutional Bodies.
  2. 2Develop the ability to understand the functioning of individual Institutions.
  3. 3Stimulate reflection on the effects produced by the functioning of individual Institutions.
  4. 4Stimulate the participation of individual citizens in the political life of the EU.

Specific Objectives

  1. 1Know the individual EU Institutions.
  2. 2Understand the tasks carried out by individual EU Institutions.
  3. 3Develop personal relationship skills and create an empathic bond.
  4. 4Understand the dynamics and interconnections between the various Institutions.
  5. 5Stimulate suggestions regarding the functioning of EU Institutions.

Target Group Description

The workshops are aimed at adults, particularly seniors, interested in the European Union and social and civic issues. No specialist knowledge is required: participants' life experiences and willingness to engage in dramatic work are fundamental elements.

Working Methods

  • Role-play / Simulation game
  • Moderated discussion
  • Group work
  • Brainstorming
  • Interactive lecture elements

Teaching Materials and Tools

  • Role cards for participants
  • Paper sheets, pens
  • Flipchart or blackboard
  • Markers
  • Clock or timer
  • Materials for writing a "Proposal for change and/or functioning of EU Institutions"

Workshop Schedule

Introduction

  • Welcome of participants.
  • Presentation of the workshop objective and rules (respect, no "right" or "wrong" answer, right to change one's mind).
  • Brief introduction to the teaching method.

Activity Description

The EU Institutions are presented seeking — as much as possible — the involvement and active participation of the participants.

Instructions — Workshop Phases

  1. 1 15 min

    Entry Questionnaire — "Europe in my memories"

    We begin with an entry questionnaire and collegial feedback (Exercise 1). Individual answers, written on a sheet, will be used to carry out a collegial review of the starting situation and to stimulate interest in the topic covered. The Brainstorming method will be used.

  2. 2 15 min

    Introduction to European Institutions (slides 1 – 2 – 3)

    The facilitator starts the presentation with a short video and the illustration of some animated slides, with a schematic and essential graphic layout, in order to highlight the composition and relationships between these institutions.

  3. 3 15 min

    Exercise 2 — two feedback cards

    Each participant is given 2 cards to complete to check and enrich their knowledge of EU Institutions. The card includes two simple exercises that help consolidate the content just presented.

  4. 4 20 min

    Plenary presentation of EU Institutions

    The facilitator presents the slides on EU Institutions to the participants in plenary and invites contributions. The presentation is carried out using animated slides containing all the data needed to more fully understand the configuration and functioning of the various Institutions considered. The facilitator will involve senior students by encouraging their participation.

  5. 5 15 min

    Exercise 3 — worksheets (3a & 3b)

    Two worksheets are provided to verify understanding of the presentation. The worksheets, set up with true/false and match the correct answer type responses, are completed in pairs. A brief collegial discussion follows to verify and consolidate learning.

  6. 6 50 min
    Exercise 4

    The Beach of Europa

    A simulation on free competition in services. The aim is to help understand the Bolkestein Directive and, more generally, how free competition in services works and the role of the Commission as Guardian of the Treaties.

    General Objective

    Allow participants to experience, in a light and guided way, how the EU intervenes when a State does not respect common rules on competition in services — as in the case of beach concessions.

    Skills the game develops
    • Understand the role of the Commission as Guardian of the Treaties.
    • Understand why the EU requires transparent tendering.
    • Recognise conflicts between rights: right to enterprise and citizens' right of access to the sea.
    • Develop critical thinking and mediation skills.
    The facilitator explains
    • What the Bolkestein Directive is, in simple terms.
    • What free competition in services means.
    • The role of the Commission as Guardian of the Treaties.

    The facilitator then assigns roles by distributing a character card to students.

    The three phases of the game
    1. 1Presentation of requests — the characters present their positions.
    2. 2Municipal Decision — a first local decision is made.
    3. 3Commission Intervention — the EU enters the scene as Guardian of the Treaties.

    The three phases are followed by a debriefing to analyse what happened, how it went, what was learned and what can be improved.

  7. 7 20 min

    Final Collegial Discussion

    A collegial discussion follows on the levels of knowledge of EU Institutions acquired by the participants.
