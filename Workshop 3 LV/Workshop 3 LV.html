Latvia · Workshop 3

Regional Policy and EU Funds

«European ABC» · Session 3 — How the EU invests in its regions. Seniors discover EU cohesion policy, the main funds and what they mean for everyday life.

Author: Nora Šņepste · Latvia

Duration 120–150 min Participants 12–25 people Audience Seniors 60+

About the Workshop

This session develops an understanding of the European Union's regional policy and EU funds.

Workshop Objectives

General Objectives

  • 1Develop understanding of the EU's regional policy and EU funds.
  • 2Develop the ability to view EU issues from different perspectives.
  • 3Encourage reflection on directions of European regional development.

Specific Objectives

  • 1Understand that the EU is a process of negotiation, compromise and choices.
  • 2Become familiar with different perspectives (Member State, candidate country).
  • 3Develop argumentation and opinion-forming skills.
  • 4Strengthen empathy and dialogue skills.

Who Is This Workshop For?

Adults, especially seniors, interested in the European Union, its regional policy and EU funding opportunities. No prior knowledge is required.

Working Methods

  • 1«Living Library» — people become «books» that can be «read» through conversation
  • 2Moderated discussion
  • 3Group work
  • 4Brainstorming
  • 5Interactive lecture elements

How the Session Unfolds (approx. 120–150 min)

  1. 1 10 min

    Introduction

    Welcome of participants and presentation of goals and rules: respect, no «right» or «wrong» answers, and the right to change one's opinion.

  2. 2 15 min

    Activity 1 — What I Know About the European Union

    In pairs, participants share memories and reflections about the European Union.

  3. 3 45 min

    Activity 2 — Interactive Lecture / Presentation

    The facilitator presents EU regional policy priorities, the available funds and examples of funded projects.

  4. 4 20 min

    Activity 3 — Discussion and Reflection

    Group discussion on what was learned and whether the EU can and should satisfy everyone.

  5. 5 30 min

    Conclusion — Seniors' Recommendations

    In groups, participants write «Senior Recommendations for Improving EU Regional Policy and Fairer Distribution and Absorption of EU Funds».

The Main EU Funds for Regional Development

Regional policy is the EU's main investment policy. For 2021–2027, €392 billion is allocated to cohesion policy — nearly one-third of the EU budget — to ensure that no region is left behind.

ERDF — European Regional Development Fund

Invests in the social and economic development of all EU regions and cities.

CF — Cohesion Fund

Supports environment and transport in the less prosperous EU countries.

ESF+ — European Social Fund Plus

Supports jobs and social inclusion across the Union.

JTF — Just Transition Fund

Supports the regions most affected by the transition to climate neutrality.

What Cohesion Policy Supports

Cohesion policy funds projects across many areas of everyday life:

  • 1Education
  • 2Employment
  • 3Energy
  • 4Environment
  • 5Single market
  • 6Research and innovation

Key EU Priorities and Territorial Focus

Cohesion policy is guided by three key priorities and pays special attention to specific types of territory.

Three key priorities

«European Green Deal» · «A Europe fit for the Digital Age» · «An economy that works for people».

Territorial focus

Border regions and cross-border cooperation (Interreg), urban areas, remote, island, mountainous or sparsely populated regions, and the outermost regions.

Cohesion and solidarity

Focus on less developed regions to reduce disparities; the Just Transition Fund supports adaptation to the climate transition; REACT-EU supported recovery after COVID-19.

Legal basis

Articles 174–178 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union.

Outcome of the Session

Working in groups, participants produce a shared document — «Senior Recommendations for Improving EU Regional Policy and Fairer Distribution and Absorption of EU Funds».
