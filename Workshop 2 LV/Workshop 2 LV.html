Latvia · Workshop 2

International Cooperation and the EU's Role in the World

«European ABC» · Session 2 — How the EU acts on the global stage. Seniors explore EU foreign policy, current priorities and the future of European integration.

Author: Nora Šņepste · Latvia

Duration 120–150 min Participants 12–25 people Audience Seniors 60+

About the Workshop

This session develops an understanding of international cooperation and the role of the European Union in the world.

Workshop Objectives

General Objectives

  • 1Develop understanding of international cooperation and the role of the EU in the world.
  • 2Develop the ability to view EU-related issues from different perspectives.
  • 3Encourage reflection on the future of European integration.

Specific Objectives

  • 1Understand that the EU is a process of negotiations, compromises and choices.
  • 2Explore different perspectives (citizen, politician, migrant, Member State, candidate country).
  • 3Develop argumentation and opinion-forming skills.
  • 4Strengthen empathy and dialogue skills.

Who Is This Workshop For?

Adults, especially seniors, interested in the role of the European Union in the world and in international cooperation. No special prior knowledge is required.

Working Methods

  • 1Moderated discussion
  • 2Group work
  • 3Brainstorming
  • 4Elements of an interactive lecture

How the Session Unfolds (approx. 120–150 min)

  1. 1 10 min

    Introduction

    Greeting participants and presenting the objectives and rules: respect, no «right» or «wrong» answers, and the right to change one's opinion.

  2. 2 15 min

    Activity 1 — The EU in International Politics

    In pairs, participants share reflections on the EU's place in the world.

  3. 3 45 min

    Activity 2 — Interactive Lecture

    An interactive lecture on EU foreign policy, the EU as a crisis manager, its current priorities and the directions shaping its future.

  4. 4 20 min

    Activity 3 — Discussion and Reflection

    Group discussion on what was learned and whether the EU is a sustainable model for today's challenges.

  5. 5 30 min

    Conclusion — Seniors' Recommendations

    In groups of five, participants write the «Senior Citizens' Recommendations for Strengthening EU Foreign Policy and Ensuring European Security».

Directions Shaping the EU's Future

In response to geopolitical challenges, the EU is moving toward closer integration. These are the main directions discussed in the lecture.

Security and Defence

Following Russia's aggression against Ukraine, the EU has significantly increased investment in common defence policy.

Strategic Autonomy

Becoming more independent in energy, technology and manufacturing to ensure economic security.

Green Deal and Energy

Continuing toward climate neutrality through renewable energy and greater efficiency.

Digital Transformation

Strong emphasis on artificial intelligence regulation and the development of robotics.

Enlargement

Ongoing discussions about admitting new member states, which would change the EU's structure.

The EU as a Crisis Manager

Over the past decade the EU has continuously faced crises that have tested its unity and leadership:

  • 1The financial crisis and threats to the euro
  • 2The migration crisis
  • 3Brexit and concerns about a domino effect
  • 4The COVID-19 pandemic
  • 5The geopolitical shock of the Russia–Ukraine war

The Five Priorities of EU Development Policy

The EU, together with its Member States, provides more than half of global development assistance, following the principles of the UN 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

People

Human development and dignified living conditions.

Prosperity

Inclusive and sustainable growth.

Planet

Environmental protection and climate action.

Peace

Democracy, human rights, migration and the rule of law.

Partnerships

Cooperation among governments, municipalities, the private sector and civil society.

Outcome of the Session

Working in small groups, participants produce a shared document — the «Senior Citizens' Recommendations for Strengthening EU Foreign Policy and Ensuring European Security».
