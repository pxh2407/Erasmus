Workshop 1 · Simulazione

The Beach of Europe

A simulation on free competition in services and the role of the European Commission as Guardian of the Treaties.

Guida: Pina Mastroieni

Duration 45 – 60 min Participants 6 – 25 Audience Adults / Seniors Type Simulation

About the Game

A simulation game for people over 60 at their first experience should be very simple, concrete, with clear roles and recognisable situations. The aim is to help understand the Bolkestein Directive and, more generally, how free competition in services works and the role of the Commission as Guardian of the Treaties.

The Bolkestein Directive — In Depth

This is the European rule at the heart of the game. Below, in simple terms: what it is, what it requires and why it also concerns Italy's beaches.

What it is

The Directive 2006/123/EC, approved in 2006, is a European law that regulates the free movement of services within the EU internal market. It establishes that:

  • service providers can work throughout the EU without unjustified obstacles;
  • States must guarantee transparency, fair competition and no discrimination.

Why it was introduced

Before the directive, many countries had different and restrictive rules. The aim is to make the services market — about 70% of the European economy — more competitive.

What it requires of States

Member States must:

  • remove unnecessary barriers to economic activities;
  • make authorisation procedures transparent;
  • ensure impartial public tenders when a resource is limited;
  • avoid automatic extensions of public concessions.

Beaches: a limited resource

Beaches are a limited natural resource and cannot be assigned «forever» without an open and transparent tender. According to the EU:

  • all operators must be able to take part in a tender;
  • citizens must enjoy free beaches and quality services.

The role of the European Commission

The Commission is the Guardian of the Treaties. If a State does not apply a directive correctly, it can:

  • send a reminder letter;
  • ask for changes to the national law;
  • bring the State before the Court of Justice.

This process is not punitive: it ensures that all European citizens have the same rights.

The case of Italy

In Italy, for many years concessions were automatically extended without tenders. The Commission opened an infringement procedure against Italy.

The thesis of the Italian government: «67% of the Italian coasts are free, so Bolkestein does not apply to existing concessions».

This claim does not hold up legally, because:

  • the EU Court assesses scarcity at the local level, not the national one;
  • many tourist municipalities have 80–90% of beaches under concession;
  • the «free» 67% also includes rocky, protected or non-bathing stretches;
  • the EU Court has already ruled that automatic renewals are unlawful.

In practice: scarcity is measured municipality by municipality. So the Bolkestein Directive applies, and concessions must be awarded through transparent and competitive procedures.

General Objective

To allow participants to experience, in a light and guided way, how the EU intervenes when a State does not respect the common rules on competition in services — as in the case of beach concessions.

Base Scenario

An imaginary municipality, «Marina Serena», must decide how to manage beach concessions. The Bolkestein Directive requires transparent and competitive tendering procedures. Some operators want to keep their historical concessions, others want to participate in new tenders. Citizens demand more free beaches. Meanwhile, the European Commission observes and intervenes if the State does not correctly apply the rules.

Game Structure (45 – 60 min)

  1. 1 5 min

    Introduction

    The facilitator explains:

    • What the Bolkestein Directive is (in simple terms)
    • What free competition in services means
    • The role of the Commission as Guardian of the Treaties
  2. 2 5 min

    Role Assignment

    Each participant receives a brief character card (see below).

  3. 3 10 min

    Phase 1 — The Requests

    Each role presents their interests:

    • who wants to keep the concession
    • who wants to participate in the tender
    • who wants more free beaches
    • who must enforce EU rules
  4. 4 10 min

    Phase 2 — The Municipality's Decision

    The "Municipality" group proposes a solution:

    • hold a public tender?
    • extend the existing concessions?
    • increase free beaches?
    • find a compromise?
  5. 5 10 min

    Phase 3 — The Commission's Intervention

    The "European Commission" group assesses whether the decision respects EU rules. It can:

    • approve
    • request modifications
    • initiate an infringement procedure (in a simplified, non-technical form)
  6. 6 10–15 min

    Final Debriefing

    Guided discussion:

    • What rights came into conflict?
    • Is it easy to balance enterprise and public interest?
    • Why does the EU intervene?
    • What have we learned about competition?

Skills the Game Develops

  • 1Understand the role of the Commission as Guardian of the Treaties.
  • 2Understand why the EU requires transparent tendering.
  • 3Recognise conflicts between rights: right to enterprise and citizens' right of access to the sea.
  • 4Develop critical thinking and mediation skills.

Role Cards

Role 1
Giovanni Rinaldi
62 years · Beach concession operator for over 30 years
Objectives
  • Keep the concession without a new tender.
  • Defend investments made over the years.
  • Convince the Municipality that continuity guarantees quality and safety.
Motivations
  • "I have always worked well and respected the rules."
  • "If a big company from outside comes, I cannot compete."
  • "My family lives from this work."
Useful Phrases
  • "You cannot cancel thirty years of experience."
  • "Competition is fine, but it must be fair."
  • "We need to protect those who have invested in the territory."
Role 2
Sara Conti
35 years · Young local entrepreneur (or European company)
Objectives
  • Obtain a real opportunity to participate in the tender.
  • Promote transparency and competition.
  • Show that new operators can bring innovation.
Motivations
  • "European rules are there to avoid favouritism."
  • "Competition can improve services and lower prices."
  • "Everyone must have the same opportunities."
Useful Phrases
  • "A public tender is a right, not a threat."
  • "An open market benefits citizens."
  • "I don't want to take anything from anyone — just have a chance."
Role 3
Maria Leone
58 years · Spokesperson for residents and beachgoers
Objectives
  • Increase free beaches.
  • Guarantee access to the sea for everyone.
  • Prevent the coastline from becoming purely a commercial affair.
Motivations
  • "The sea is a common good."
  • "Too many concessions limit citizens' freedom."
  • "There must be a balance between enterprise and public interest."
Useful Phrases
  • "We cannot privatise everything."
  • "Families must be able to go to the beach without paying."
  • "Nature must be protected."
Role 4
Assessore Luca Ferri
55 years · Head of concessions and local decisions
Objectives
  • Find a compromise between operators, citizens and EU rules.
  • Avoid conflicts and protests.
  • Avoid triggering an infringement procedure.
Motivations
  • "We must respect both national and European law."
  • "The Municipality must guarantee services and public access."
  • "We cannot disappoint everyone."
Useful Phrases
  • "Let us look for a shared solution."
  • "European regulations are not optional."
  • "We need a balance between different rights."
Role 5
Isabelle Moreau
48 years · Official of the Directorate-General for Internal Market
Objectives
  • Verify that the State correctly applies the Bolkestein Directive.
  • Guarantee competition and transparency.
  • Intervene if the rules are not respected.
Motivations
  • "The Commission is the Guardian of the Treaties."
  • "The rules apply to all Member States."
  • "Competition protects consumers."
Useful Phrases
  • "Is your decision in line with European standards?"
  • "We may ask for clarification or open a procedure."
  • "The goal is not to punish, but to enforce the common rules."
Role 6
Peter Schneider
60 years · German tourist who often visits Italy
Objectives
  • Enjoy quality services at fair prices.
  • Find accessible beaches.
  • Understand why there are differences between countries.
Motivations
  • "In other European countries access to the sea is freer."
  • "Competition can improve the offer."
  • "I am a European consumer, not just a tourist."
Useful Phrases
  • "I would like more price transparency."
  • "Why are some beaches entirely private?"
  • "I care about quality, not who manages it."
