Latvia · Workshop 1

The EU's Four Freedoms

«European ABC» · Session 1 — Free movement of persons, goods, services and capital. A simulation game helping seniors discover how the EU's freedoms benefit everyday life.

Author: Guntars Ratenieks · Latvia

Duration 80–100 min Participants 4 groups Audience Seniors 60+

About the Workshop

The aim of this session is to reduce Euroscepticism among seniors by using practical and personally meaningful examples of the benefits of the European Union's four freedoms — the free movement of persons, goods, services and capital.

Workshop Objectives

Main Objective

  • ★Reduce Euroscepticism among the senior social group through practical, personally meaningful examples.

Sub-objectives

  • 1Promote positive examples and benefits of European Union policies.
  • 2Demonstrate the benefits for seniors from the free movement of persons, goods, services and capital.

Who Is This Workshop For?

Seniors aged 60+. No specialist knowledge of the EU is required — the session builds on participants' everyday experiences and personal examples.

Working Methods

  • 1Interactive simulation game
  • 2Group work with role and situation cards
  • 3Moderated discussion
  • 4Voting (yes / no cards)
  • 5Individual and group reflection
  • 6Interactive screen (maps, price comparisons, short videos, statistics)

The EU's Four Freedoms

The single market is built on four freedoms. The facilitator briefly introduces each one before the simulation game.

Free movement of persons

EU citizens can travel, live, study and work in any member state.

Free movement of goods

Products move freely across the EU.

Free movement of services

Services can be provided across borders.

Free movement of capital

Money, investments and payments move freely.

How the Session Unfolds (approx. 80–100 min)

  1. 1 10–15 min

    Introduction

    The facilitator introduces the four freedoms and asks whether EU decisions affect everyday life even after 60. Participants complete the sentence "When I hear ‘European Union', I think of…" and share their views.

  2. 2 45–55 min

    Simulation Game

    Participants are divided into four groups. Each group receives a situation, role cards and a task, then works through an everyday situation where the EU's four freedoms apply.

  3. 3 15–20 min

    Presentations and Group Discussion

    Each group briefly presents its situation, identifies the main benefits for seniors and shares one conclusion: "What do I personally gain from the European Union?"

  4. 4 10 min

    Reflection and Conclusion

    Guiding questions: What surprised me today? Did I learn something new? Has my attitude toward the EU changed?

The Simulation Game — «Life in Europe: How Do EU Freedoms Help Me?»

Four groups, four everyday situations. Each group discusses its situation, completes a task and answers the guiding questions.

1

Situation 1 — «Grandchildren Living in Ireland»

Free movement of persons

Anna is 68. Her daughter works in Ireland and Anna wants to visit her grandchildren regularly.

Discuss: What difficulties would exist without the EU? What benefits do seniors gain?

2

Situation 2 — «Cheaper Medicines and Safer Products»

Free movement of goods

Jānis regularly buys medicines and household goods produced in other EU countries.

Find examples: How does the EU affect prices and quality? How do EU standards protect people?

3

Situation 3 — «Pension and Banking in Europe»

Free movement of capital

Pēteris receives his pension in Latvia, while his son lives in Germany.

Evaluate: What are the benefits of a unified financial system? What problems would exist without common rules?

4

Situation 4 — «Medical Treatment in Another EU Country»

Free movement of services

Marija needs eye surgery. She learns that in certain cases treatment can be received in another EU country.

Discuss: What benefits do patients receive? How does the EU expand access to services?

Expected Outcomes

  • 1Better understand the significance of the EU's four freedoms.
  • 2Identify specific benefits for seniors.
  • 3Evaluate negative stereotypes about the EU more critically.
  • 4Feel a stronger personal connection to the European Union.
