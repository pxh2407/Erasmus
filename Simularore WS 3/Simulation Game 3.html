Workshop 3 · Simulazione

Simulation Game — Let's Build the EU Budget

The second part of the workshop is a simulation game in three activities. Through play, senior participants activate their own ideas, experience the European decision-making process first-hand and concretely understand how EU funds work.

Coordinatrici: Lilla Trischitta e Pina Mastroieni

Tutor: Antonio Catalfamo

Duration~80 min Participants20 – 25 AudienceAdults / Seniors
1

Opening Brainstorming — "Where does Europe's money come from?"

15–20 min

Goal: Activate prior knowledge and introduce the sources of the EU budget (GNI, VAT, customs duties, non-recycled plastic, other revenue).

Materials: A3 sheets, markers, sticky notes.

Phases

  1. Initial prompt (2 min) — read aloud a sentence from the document: "The EU budget funds common priorities, promoting growth, competitiveness, social cohesion and sustainability…" then ask: "In your opinion… where does the money Europe uses for all this come from?"
  2. Free idea collection (5 min) — participants write a possible source of revenue on a sticky note (even imaginative ones).
  3. Guided grouping (8 min) — ideas are grouped into the 5 real sources: GNI share, VAT percentage, customs duties, non-recycled plastic contribution, other revenue (fines, extra-EU contributions…).
  4. Mini-debrief (3 min) — Which sources surprised you? Which seem fairest? Which most effective?
2

Simulation Game — "Let's Build the EU Budget"

45–60 min

Goal: Understand how the EU budget is shared out and why some headings receive more funds.

Materials: 7 cards (one per spending heading) and 100 tokens = the total budget.

The 7 spending headings (with share of the budget)

Cohesion & values34%
Natural resources & environment30%
Single market & innovation15%
Neighbourhood & the world9%
EU public administration7%
Migration & border management3%
Security & defence2%

Phases

  1. Group formation (2 min) — the class is divided into 7 groups, each representing a heading.
  2. Initial mandate (5 min) — each group receives the description of its heading and a card with 3 projects to fund.
  3. First negotiation (10 min) — Parliament and Council (played by two volunteers) distribute the 100 tokens among the headings. Each group must convince the others it needs more funds.
  4. Final approval (5 min) — Parliament votes on the final proposal (simple majority).
  5. Debriefing (10 min) — Which headings received most funds and why? Was it hard to reach agreement? Does it feel like a democratic process? (link: "DEMOCRATIC — approved by co-decision").
3

Mini Simulation Game — "Which EU fund pays for what?"

20–25 min

Goal: Help participants recognise the EU Structural Funds and what they are for.

14 "project" cards

  • Training course for unemployed people over 50
  • Renovation of a historic town centre
  • Creation of a cycle path
  • Support for young farmers
  • Restoration of a fishing port
  • Digitalisation of SMEs
  • Protection of a natural area
  • Programme against child poverty
  • Sicily–Malta cross-border cooperation
  • Development of a local farmers' market
  • Energy upgrade of a municipal school
  • Home care services for frail elderly people
  • Promotion of rural tourism in inland areas
  • Modernisation of the small-scale artisanal fishing fleet

Phases

  1. Card distribution (2 min) — each group receives 2 projects.
  2. Matching (10 min) — groups decide which fund pays for each project, justifying their choice with sentences from the document.
  3. Collective comparison (8 min) — each group presents its choices; the facilitator confirms or corrects.
  4. Debrief (5 min) — Which funds feel closest to your daily life? Which projects struck you most?

Project cards: 3 projects for each of the 7 groups

Negotiation role-play: "Let's Build the EU Budget"

Setting: The European Parliament and the Council must approve how the budget is shared out. Each group has 3 projects to fund. The total budget is 100 tokens.

President of Parliament

"Good morning everyone. Today we must decide how to distribute the European Union's budget. Each group has three projects to propose. We will listen to you one at a time."

Cohesion & values

"We ask for 20 tokens. Our projects improve daily life: a centre for active seniors, an intergenerational programme, the regeneration of a district. They reduce inequalities and strengthen the community." Single market: "I understand, but 20 is a lot." · Environment: "Urban regeneration also has an environmental impact, so we support you."

Natural resources & environment

"We ask for 25 tokens. The climate can't wait: urban gardens, reforestation, farmers' green transition. These are investments that protect the future." Security & defence: "Reforestation reduces fire risk, so it's also security." · Public administration: "But 25 is a quarter of the budget…"

Single market & innovation

"We ask for 15 tokens: public Wi-Fi, educational robotics for adults, digitalisation of SMEs. Without innovation, Europe falls behind." Cohesion: "Wi-Fi in small towns helps us too." · Environment: "Digitalisation reduces waste and travel."

Neighbourhood & the world

"We ask for 10 tokens: Sicily–Tunisia twinning, humanitarian aid, literacy for migrant women. Europe is stronger when it supports its neighbours." Migration: "Your literacy project helps us manage integration better." · Defence: "Humanitarian aid reduces instability at the borders."

Migration & border management

"We ask for 8 tokens: a first-reception centre, training for officers, a digital system for flows. We need humanity, but also organisation." Cohesion: "The reception centre has a strong social impact." · Single market: "The digital system is innovation."

Security & defence

"We ask for 7 tokens: a campaign against scams targeting seniors, stronger civil protection, cybersecurity training. Security is a common good." Cohesion: "Digital scams hit seniors hardest, so we support you." · Environment: "Civil protection is essential against fires."

EU public administration

"We ask for 5 tokens: accessible public offices, training of officials, digital archives. Good administration serves everyone." Single market: "Digitalising archives is essential." · Cohesion: "Accessibility is a right."

First distribution proposal

Parliament proposes: Cohesion 18 · Environment 23 · Innovation 14 · Neighbourhood 9 · Migration 8 · Security 6 · Administration 5. Total: 83 (17 tokens missing). The Council proposes to share the remaining 17: +5 Environment, +5 Cohesion, +3 Innovation, +2 Security, +2 Migration.

Final agreement

After discussion, Parliament and Council approve: Cohesion 22 · Environment 28 · Innovation 14 · Neighbourhood 9 · Migration 10 · Security 9 · Administration 8 = 100 tokens.

Debriefing

  • Which groups pushed the hardest?
  • Which projects convinced you most?
  • Did unexpected events change the priorities?
  • Does it feel like a democratic process?
  • What did you discover about the EU budget?

Conclusion of the module

With these three activities, senior students: activate their personal knowledge and opinions (brainstorming), experience the European decision-making process (simulation game) and concretely understand the EU funds (mini-game).
