Germany · Workshop 1

The European Union — a Journey through Time

An interactive journey through the origins and development of the European Union, up to the present day — to feel part of the European community.

Authors: Barbara Petersen, Renate Kronemann, Sabine Perisic, Achim Kolbe-Dierks

Duration 90 – 120 min Participants up to 19 Audience Ages 50+

About the Workshop

Participants expand their knowledge about the origins of the EU and its development up to today, and come to see themselves as part of the European community. The heart of the workshop is a timeline built together, step by step.

What the Workshop Develops

  • 1Activate and recall existing knowledge about the EU.
  • 2Place in chronological order the events that built European integration.
  • 3Highlight the guiding principles of the European community.
  • 4Deepen factual knowledge: treaties, accessions, dates.
  • 5Reflect critically on the development of the EU and its consequences.
  • 6Share personal experiences connected to the EU.

How the Workshop Unfolds (90 – 120 min)

  1. 110 min

    Warm-up round

    Each participant says one word they associate with the European idea.

  2. 215 min

    The milestones

    The eight key milestones are presented on a timeline laid out on the table.

  3. 325 min

    The 21 events

    In small groups, participants place 21 event cards in chronological order and present them.

  4. 415 min

    Memory games

    Memory games on the flags of EU countries and famous landmarks; the symbolism of the European flag is explained.

  5. 510 min

    Flags and accessions

    Country flags are placed on the timeline by their year of accession, with a discussion of the historical context.

  6. 610–15 min

    Final round

    Each participant shares what personal benefit they see in being an EU citizen.

The Timeline — milestones & events

From the Schuman Declaration to today. The milestones are highlighted; the other entries are the further events that shaped the Union.

Milestone  ·  Event

  1. 1950
    Milestone

    Schuman Declaration

    On 9 May, French Foreign Minister Robert Schuman proposes the creation of the ECSC. Today this day is celebrated as Europe Day.

  2. 1952

    European Court of Justice

    The Court of Justice of the European Union, based in Luxembourg, is established.

  3. 1953

    Treaty of Paris

    The European Coal and Steel Community (ECSC), also known as the 'Montanunion', enters into force.

  4. 1958
    Milestone

    Treaties of Rome

    The treaties establishing the European Economic Community (EEC) and Euratom enter into force.

  5. 1967
    Milestone

    Merger Treaty

    The ECSC, EEC and Euratom merge into the European Communities (EC).

  6. 1970

    European Political Cooperation

    EC countries set up European Political Cooperation (EPC) to better represent their foreign-policy interests.

  7. 1979
    Milestone

    European Elections

    The first direct elections to the European Parliament are introduced.

  8. 1985

    Greenland

    After a referendum, Greenland withdraws from the EC but remains associated with it.

  9. 1986

    Europe's Flag and Anthem

    The European flag is hoisted for the first time and the 'Ode to Joy' becomes the European anthem.

  10. 1987

    Erasmus

    The Erasmus programme is launched.

  11. 1987

    Single European Act

    The Single European Act enters into force, aiming to complete the internal market by 1 January 1993.

  12. 1992

    EU Ecolabel

    The EU Ecolabel — the 'Euroflower' — becomes the official environmental label of the EU.

  13. 1993
    Milestone

    Treaty of Maastricht

    The European Union (EU) is created, merging the EEC, ECSC and Euratom, with cooperation in foreign, defence, police and justice policy.

  14. 1995
    Milestone

    Schengen Agreement

    The agreement abolishing checks on persons at the EU's internal borders enters into force.

  15. 1998
    Milestone

    European Central Bank

    The ECB is founded in Frankfurt, tasked with ensuring price stability in the euro area.

  16. 1998

    Europol

    Europol, the European Police Office, is founded in The Hague to fight serious crime and terrorism.

  17. 1999

    Euro — Virtual Currency

    The euro is introduced as a virtual currency unit, replacing the ECU.

  18. 2000

    Charter of Fundamental Rights

    The Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union is adopted.

  19. 2000

    Motto 'United in Diversity'

    The Commission chooses 'United in Diversity' as the motto of the EU.

  20. 2001

    AGE Platform Europe

    The European umbrella organisation representing the interests of older people is founded.

  21. 2002
    Milestone

    Introduction of Euro Cash

    The euro becomes the official cash currency in 12 EU countries.

  22. 2003

    Treaty of Nice

    The Treaty of Nice strengthens the European Parliament and sets institutional reforms in motion.

  23. 2009

    Treaty of Lisbon

    The structure and working methods of the EU are reformed; it is the EU's current treaty basis.

  24. 2009

    Euro Sovereign Debt Crisis

    High public debt and falling tax revenues push some EU countries towards bankruptcy.

  25. 2009

    EU Recovery Package

    Financially threatened member states are supported through the euro rescue package.

  26. 2012

    Nobel Peace Prize

    The EU is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for advancing peace, reconciliation, democracy and human rights.

  27. 2015

    Peak of the Refugee Crisis

    In a short time over a million people reach Europe; the countries on the EU's external borders are overwhelmed.

  28. 2020

    Brexit

    After a 2016 referendum and years of negotiation, the United Kingdom leaves the EU, which now has 27 members.

  29. 2020

    Corona Pandemic

    Health systems are overburdened; lockdowns put the economy and social life into crisis.

Who is Who?

Three names that are easy to confuse.

Council of Europe

An international organisation (not an EU institution), based in Strasbourg, devoted to human rights, democracy and the rule of law.

European Council

Defines the EU's general objectives and priorities. Members: the heads of state or government, plus the Presidents of the European Council and the Commission.

Council of the European Union

Negotiates and adopts EU legislation (usually together with the Parliament). Members: the relevant ministers of the member states.
