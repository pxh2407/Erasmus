Poland · Workshop 1

Europe at a Crossroads

A drama and role-play workshop exploring EU challenges and decisions, designed for seniors to reflect on European integration from multiple perspectives.

Author: Barbara Janic · Fundacja "Aktywni XXI"

Duration 120–150 min Participants 12–25 people Audience Seniors

About the Workshop

Participants explore the European Union through personal memories, perspective-taking and drama. By stepping into different roles they discover the complexity of EU decision-making and strengthen their sense of civic agency.

Workshop Objectives

General Objectives

  • 1Develop civic awareness of how the European Union functions.
  • 2Build the ability to look at EU challenges from multiple perspectives.
  • 3Encourage reflection on the future of European integration.

Specific Objectives

  • 1Understand that the EU is a process of negotiations, compromises and choices.
  • 2Learn about different viewpoints: citizen, politician, migrant, Member State, candidate country.
  • 3Develop argumentation and opinion-forming skills.
  • 4Strengthen empathy and dialogue skills.

Who Is This Workshop For?

The workshop is designed for adults, especially seniors, interested in the European Union and civic affairs. No specialist knowledge is required — what matters is life experience and a willingness to engage in drama work.

Working Methods

  • 1Drama / role play
  • 2Moderated discussion
  • 3Group work
  • 4Brainstorming
  • 5Elements of interactive lecture

How the Workshop Unfolds (120–150 min)

  1. 1

    Introduction

    Welcome participants, present the workshop objective and ground rules. Brief introduction to the drama method.

    10 min

  2. 2

    Activity 1 — "The Europe I Remember"

    Participants work in pairs, sharing memories and reflections on the EU. Willing pairs share a short statement with the group.

    15 min

  3. 3

    Activity 2 — Drama: "EU Future Summit"

    Participants draw roles and debate three key challenges: migration, EU enlargement and Brexit as a lesson. The facilitator moderates a simulated EU summit.

    60 min

  4. 4

    Activity 3 — Discussion and Reflection

    Group discussion after the drama: What was most difficult? Did I change my mind? Can the EU please everyone?

    20 min

  5. 5

    Summary — "Seniors' Declaration"

    Together the group writes the "Seniors' Declaration on the Future of the European Union" — a 1–2 page document with the participants' most important conclusions, values and demands.

    30 min

Role Cards

Each participant (or small group) draws one card. They do not need to "act" — they simply speak from that role's perspective. Print on A5 or A4, preferably on coloured paper.

1

Polish Citizen (Senior)

You are a senior living in Poland. You remember the times before EU accession and the changes after 2004.

Security · Stability · Preserving national identity · Support for regions and seniors

2

Citizen of the "Old EU"

You are a senior from a country that has been in the EU from the very beginning (e.g. Germany, France).

Economic stability · Common rules · Responsibility of all countries

3

European Union Politician

You must think about the interests of the entire EU, not just those of a single country.

Seeking compromises · Mitigating conflicts · Thinking long-term

4

Migrant

You came to Europe from a country affected by war, crisis or climate change.

Safety · Work · Respect

5

Representative of a Candidate Country

You represent a country that wants to join the European Union.

The EU stands for peace and development · Shared values · A better future for young people

6

EU Sceptic

You believe the EU has too much influence on the lives of countries.

Too many regulations · Loss of sovereignty · Brexit as an example

Problem Cards

The facilitator draws 2–4 cards during the drama, depending on the pace of the debate. Each card introduces a new challenge for the group to discuss.

1

"A large number of migrants are arriving in Europe."

2

"One country is considering leaving the EU."

3

"Climate change is causing a food crisis."

4

"A new country wants to join the EU."

5

"Citizens are losing trust in EU institutions."
